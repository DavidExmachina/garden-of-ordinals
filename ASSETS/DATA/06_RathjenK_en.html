Definition

  Rathjen's Capital \( \Psi \) refers to another ordinal collapsing function proposed by Michael Rathjen in 1994[1]. The whole ordinal system consists of 4 functions: \( \varphi_\alpha(\beta) \), \( \Omega_\alpha \), \( \Xi(\alpha) \) and \( \Psi_\alpha^\beta(\gamma) \) and described and defined as follows.

The function \( \varphi_\alpha(\beta) \)

  \( \varphi_\alpha(\beta) \) denotes Veblen Function. It's defined as follows as shown in the Help menu in the "Veblen Function" interface:

  01. \( \varphi_0(\beta)=\omega^\beta \)

  02. For a non-zero ordinal \( \alpha \), \( \varphi_{\alpha}(\beta) \) returns the \( (1+\beta) \)-th common fixed point of all of the functions \( \gamma\mapsto\varphi_{\alpha'}(\gamma) \) for \( \alpha'<\alpha \).

The function \( \Omega_\alpha \)

  \( \Omega_\alpha \) denotes the \( (1+\alpha) \)-th infinite cardinal. Note that \( \Omega_0 \) is defined as \( 0 \) in the paper.

The function \( \Xi(\alpha) \)

  \( \Xi(\alpha) \) denotes the least element in the Mahlo Cardinal hierarchy \( M^\alpha \), which can be considered the "least Mahlo function". It's defined as follows: (\( \alpha<\Gamma_{K+1} \))

  \( \Xi(\alpha)=min(M^\alpha\cup\{K\}) \)


  The class \( M^\alpha \) is defined as follows:

  \( M^0=Lim\cap K \)

  \( \begin{align} M^\alpha=\{\kappa| & \kappa\in Reg\cap K \\ & C(\alpha,\kappa)\cap K=\kappa \\ & \alpha\in C(\alpha,\kappa) \\ & \forall\alpha'\in C(\alpha,\kappa)\cap\alpha(M^{\alpha'}\in Sta_\kappa) \}\ (\alpha>0) \end{align} \)

  Where \( Lim \) denotes the class of limit ordinals, \( Reg \) denotes the class of regular cardinals and \( Sta_\kappa \) denotes the class of sets stationary in \( \kappa \). You can check the definition of stationary sets in the Help menu in the "Rathjen's Little \( \psi \)" interface.


  The set \( C(\alpha,\beta) \) is defined as follows:

  \( \begin{align} C_0(\alpha,\beta) := & \beta\cup\{0,K\} \\ C_{n+1}(\alpha,\beta) := & \{\gamma|\gamma=\gamma_0+\gamma_1\land\gamma_0,\gamma_1\in C_n(\alpha,\beta)\} \\ \cup & \{\gamma|\gamma=\varphi_{\alpha'}(\beta')\land\alpha',\beta'\in C_n(\alpha,\beta)\} \\ \cup & \{\gamma|\gamma=\Omega_{\alpha'}\land\alpha'\in C_n(\alpha,\beta)\cap K\} \\ \cup & \{\gamma|\gamma=\Xi(\alpha')\land\alpha'\in C_n(\alpha,\beta)\cap\alpha\} \\ \cup & \{\gamma|\gamma=\Psi_{\alpha'}^{\beta'}(\gamma')\land\alpha'\in Reg\land\beta'\le\gamma'<\alpha\land\alpha',\beta',\gamma'\in C_n(\alpha,\beta) \} \\ C(\alpha,\beta) := & \bigcup_{n<\omega}{C_n(\alpha,\beta)}=C_0(\alpha,\beta)\cup C_1(\alpha,\beta)\cup C_2(\alpha,\beta)\cup\cdots\cup C_m(\alpha,\beta)\cup\cdots \end{align} \)


  From the definition above, we can see that it introduces an extension of Mahlo Cardinals:

  \( M^0 \) denotes the class of limit ordinals (less than \( K \)).

  \( M^1 \) denotes the class of Inaccessible Cardinals due to the presence of the function \( \Omega_\alpha \). The reason why this function is included is probably because Inaccessible Cardinals are equivalent to \( \Pi_0^1- \)indescribable cardinals[2].

  \( M^2 \) denotes the class of Mahlo Cardinals. Not only does this contain the least Mahlo Cardinal \( M \), but it also contains larger Mahlo Cardinals like \( M_2 \), \( M_3 \) and so on.

  \( M^3 \) denotes the class of "Hyper-Mahlo Cardinals". It contains Mahlo Cardinals \( \kappa \) that the set of all Mahlo Cardinals less than \( \kappa \) is a stationary set in \( \kappa \).

  \( M^4 \) denotes the class of "Hyper-Hyper-Mahlo Cardinals". It contains Hyper-Mahlo Cardinals \( \kappa \) that the set of all Hyper-Mahlo Cardinals less than \( \kappa \) is a stationary set in \( \kappa \) and so on.


  As a consequence, \( \Xi(1)=I \) and \( \Xi(2)=M \). Sometimes we denote \( \Xi(3) \) as \( N \).

The function \( \Psi_\alpha^\beta(\gamma) \)

  \( \Psi_\alpha^\beta(\gamma) \) denotes the collapsing function, which is the core of this ordinal system. It's defined as follows: (\( \alpha\in Reg \) and \( \beta\le\gamma \) and \( \gamma<\Gamma_{K+1} \))

  \( \begin{align} \Psi_\alpha^\beta(\gamma)=min(\{\delta| & \delta\in M^\beta\cap\alpha \\ & C(\gamma,\delta)\cap\alpha=\delta \\ & \alpha,\gamma\in C(\gamma,\delta) \}\cup\{\alpha\}) \end{align} \)


  As you can see, this OCF takes 3 ordinals as input instead of 2 like most of other OCFs. If \( \beta>0 \), then \( \Psi_\alpha^\beta(\gamma) \) always return a regular cardinal. Moreover, since both \( \Psi_\alpha^\beta(\gamma) \) and \( \Xi(\beta) \) returns an element of \( M^\beta \), it can represent larger Inaccessible Cardinals, Mahlo Cardinals, "Hyper-Mahlo Cardinals" and so on. As a consequence, \( \Psi_{\Xi(2)}^1(3)=I_2 \), \( \Psi_{\Xi(2)}^1(4)=I_3 \), \( \Psi_{\Xi(3)}^2(4)=M_2 \), \( \Psi_{\Xi(3)}^2(5)=M_3 \), \( \Psi_{\Xi(4)}^3(5)=N_2 \), \( \Psi_{\Xi(4)}^3(6)=N_3 \) and so on.

  Also, it seems it's possible that \( \Psi_\alpha^\beta(\gamma)=\alpha \). In fact, this is true, and same goes for the function \( \Xi(\alpha) \). When this happens, we say that the function "fails to collapse". As a consequence, most of the time, there's a least \( \gamma \) so that \( \Psi_\alpha^\beta(\gamma) \) doesn't fail to collapse.

  The most challenging part of this OCF is that \( \Psi_\alpha^\beta(\gamma) \) doesn't monotonically increase in \( \gamma \), meaning that it's easy to overlook some ordinals when analyzing this OCF.

  For example, we might expect this at first glance: (Where \( supA \) denotes the least ordinal greater or equal to all of the elements in \( A \))

  \( \Psi_\Omega^0(\Omega+1)=sup\{\Psi_\Omega^0(\Omega),\varphi_{\Psi_\Omega^0(\Omega)}(1),\varphi_{\varphi_{\Psi_\Omega^0(\Omega)}(1)}(0),\varphi_{\varphi_{\varphi_{\Psi_\Omega^0(\Omega)}(1)}(0)}(0),\cdots\} \)

  But in fact:

  \( \Psi_\Omega^0(\Omega+1)=sup\{\Psi_\Omega^0(\Omega),\Psi_\Omega^0(\Psi_\Omega^0(\Omega)),\Psi_\Omega^0(\Psi_\Omega^0(\Psi_\Omega^0(\Omega))),\Psi_\Omega^0(\Psi_\Omega^0(\Psi_\Omega^0(\Psi_\Omega^0(\Omega)))),\cdots\} \)

  As a result, it becomes extremely difficult to determine whether an infinite sequence approaches to ordinals represented in this OCF in general. With all the complexities above, Rathjen's Capital \( \Psi \) becomes one of the hardest OCFs to understand.

The Weakly Compact Cardinal \( K \)

  The Weakly Compact Cardinal \( K \) is the key to this OCF. There are multiple definitions of this cardinal. For example, Weakly Compact Cardinals are equivalent to \( \Pi_1^1- \)indescribable cardinals. However, almost all of the definitions require high-level set theory concepts. Since it requires a long article to explain this cardinal clearly, this article doesn't aim to explain this cardinal. One thing is certain, the immense size of this cardinal allows itself to be used to construct high-level Mahlo Cardinals. By the way, ZFC can't determine the existence of Weakly Compact Cardinals.


  The ordinal \( \Psi_\Omega^0(\varepsilon_{K+1}) \) is called the Large Rathjen ordinal (LRO). It's the upper bound of the PTO of \( KP+\Pi_3-reflection \).

  07.01. If \( a_0<b_0 \), return \( a_1<b \)

  07.03. If \( a_0=_*b_0 \), return \( a_1<b_1 \)

  08. If \( a=\Omega_{a_0}\land b=\Omega_{b_0}\land a_0,b_0\in T_m \), return \( a_0<b_0 \)

  09. If \( a=\Xi(a_0)\land b=\Xi(b_0)\land a_0,b_0\in T_m \), then:

  09.01. If \( a_0<b_0 \), return \( a_0\in C(b_0,b) \)

  10.01.04. Else if \( b_1<a_1\land b_1\in C(a_1,a) \), return \( \mathbb{T} \)

  10.01.05. Else, return \( \mathbb{F} \)

  10.02. Else if \( a_0\neg_*b_0\land a_1\neg_*b_1\land a_2\neg_*b_2 \), return \( \neg b<a \)

  10.03. Else, return \( \mathbb{F} \)

  11. If \( a=K\land b\neq K \), then:

  11.01. If \( b=\varphi_{b_0}(b_1)\land b_0,b_1\in T_m \), return \( (a<b_0\vee b_1\neq\overline{0})\land(\neg b_0<a\vee a<b_1) \)

  11.02. If \( b=\Omega_{b_0}\land b_0\in T_m \), return \( a<b_0 \)

  11.03. If \( b=\Xi(b_0)\land b_0\in T_m \), return \( \mathbb{F} \)

  11.04. If \( b=\Psi_{b_0}^{b_1}(b_2)\land b_0,b_1,b_2\in T_m \), return \( \mathbb{F} \)

  12. If \( a\neq K\land b=K \), then:

  12.01. If \( a=\varphi_{a_0}(a_1)\land a_0,a_1\in T_m \), return \( a_0<b\land a_1<b \)

  12.02. If \( a=\Omega_{a_0}\land a_0\in T_m \), return \( a_0<b \)

  12.03. If \( a=\Xi(a_0)\land a_0\in T_m \), return \( \mathbb{T} \)

  12.04. If \( a=\Psi_{a_0}^{a_1}(a_2)\land a_0,a_1,a_2\in T_m \), return \( \mathbb{T} \)

  13. If \( a=\varphi_{a_0}(a_1)\land(b=\Omega_{b_0}\vee b=\Xi(b_0)\vee b=\Psi_{b_0}^{b_1}(b_2))\land a_0,a_1,b_0,b_1,b_2\in T_m \), then:

  13.01. Return \( a_0<b\land a_1<b \)

  14. If \( (a=\Omega_{a_0}\vee a=\Xi(a_0)\vee a=\Psi_{a_0}^{a_1}(a_2))\land b=\varphi_{b_0}(b_1)\land a_0,a_1,a_2,b_0,b_1\in T_m \), then:

  14.01. Return \( (a<b_0\vee b_1\neq\overline{0})\land(\neg b_0<a\vee a<b_1) \)

  15. If \( a=\Omega_{a_0}\land b=\Xi(b_0)\land a_0,b_0\in T_m \), return \( a_0<b \)

  17.01. If \( b_0=\Omega_{b_3}\land b_3\in T_m \), return \( a<b_0 \)

  17.02. Else, return \( a_0<b \)

  18.02. Else, return \( a<b_0 \)

  19. If \( a=\Xi(a_0)\land b=\Psi_{b_0}^{b_1}(b_2)\land a_0,b_0,b_1,b_2\in T_m \), return \( a<b_0\land(\neg a_0<b_2\vee a_0\in C(b_2,b)) \)

  20. If \( a=\Psi_{a_0}^{a_1}(a_2)\land b=\Xi(b_0)\land a_0,a_1,a_2,b_0\in T_m \), return \( \neg b<a_0\vee(b_0<a_2\land\neg b_2\in C(a_2,a)) \)

  \( (a\in T_m,b\in T_m)\mapsto a>b\in\{\mathbb{T},\mathbb{F}\} \)

  \( (a\in T_m,b\in T_m)\mapsto a\le b\in\{\mathbb{T},\mathbb{F}\} \)

Normal Form

  02. If \( \neg a<b \), return \( a \)

Operations

  We define addition \( Add \) as follows:

  \( (a\in T,b\in T,c\in T\cup\{0\})\mapsto Add(a,b,c)\in T \) (Note that \( 0 \) is a numerical value and it's DIFFERENT from the symbol \( \overline{0} \))

  01. If \( a=\overline{0} \), return \( b \)

  02. If \( a\neq\overline{0}\land b=\overline{0} \), return \( a \)

  03. If \( a=a_0+a_1\land b=b_0+b_1\land a_0,b_0\in T_s\land a_1,b_1\in T_m\setminus\{\overline{0}\} \), then:

  03.01. If \( c=0 \), then:

  03.01.01. If \( a_0<b_0 \), return \( b \)

  03.01.02. If \( \neg a_0<b_0 \), return \( a_0+Add(a_1,b,0) \)

  03.02. If \( c\in T \), then:

  03.02.01. If \( \neg a<c \), return \( a \)

  03.02.02. Else if \( c=c_0+c_1\land c_0\in T_s\land c_1\in T_m\setminus\{\overline{0}\}\land a_0=b_0\land b_0=c_0 \), return \( a_0+Add(a_1,b_1,c_1) \)

  03.02.03. Else, return \( Add(a,b,0) \)

  \( (a\in T)\mapsto Suc(a)\in T\setminus\{\overline{0}\} \)

  Return \( Add(a,\overline{1}) \)


  We define the tail function \( t \) as follows:

  \( (a\in T,b\in T,c\in\{0,1,2\})\mapsto t_b^c(a)\in T_m \) (Note that it doesn't always return a normal term if \( c=2 \))

  01. If \( a=\overline{0} \), return \( \overline{0} \)

  02. If \( a<b \), then:

  02.01. If \( c=0 \), return \( a \)

  02.02. If \( c=1 \), return \( \overline{0} \)

  02.03. If \( c=2 \), return \( b \)

  03. If \( a=b \), then:

  03.01. If \( c=0 \), return \( \overline{0} \)

  03.02. If \( c\neq0 \), return \( a \)

  04. If \( b<a \), then:

  04.01. If \( a=a_0+a_1\land a_0\in T_s\land a_1\in T_m\setminus\{\overline{0}\} \), then:

  04.01.01. If \( c=0 \), return \( t_b^c(a_1) \)

  04.01.02. If \( c\neq0 \), return \( a_0+t_b^c(a_1) \)

  04.02. Else if \( c=1 \), return \( a \)

  04.03. Else if \( (a=\Xi(a_0)\vee a=\Psi_{a_0}^{a_1}(a_2))\land a_0,a_1,a_2\in T_m \), return \( a \)

  04.04. Else if \( a=\varphi_{a_0}(a_1)\land a_0,a_1\in T_m \), then:

  04.04.01. If \( Cof(a_1)<\overline{\omega} \), then:

  04.04.01.01. If \( c=0 \), return \( t_b^c(a_0) \)

  04.04.01.02. If \( c\neq0 \), return \( \varphi_{t_b^c(a_0)}(a_1) \)

  04.04.02. If \( \neg Cof(a_1)<\overline{\omega} \), then:

  04.04.02.01. If \( c=0 \), return \( t_b^c(a_1) \)

  04.04.02.02. If \( c\neq0 \), return \( \varphi_{a_0}(t_b^c(a_1)) \)

  04.05. Else if \( a=\Omega_{a_0}\land a_0\in T_m \), then:

  04.05.01. If \( c=0 \), return \( t_b^c(a_0) \)

  04.05.02. If \( c\neq0 \), return \( \Omega_{t_b^c(a_0)} \)


  We define the \( \Omega- \)tail function \( \Omega t \) as follows:

  \( (a\in T)\mapsto\Omega t(a)\in T \)

  01. If \( a=\overline{0}\vee a=K \), return \( a \)

  02. If \( a=a_0+a_1\land a_0\in T_s\land a_1\in T_m\setminus\{\overline{0}\} \), return \( Max(\Omega t(a_0),\Omega t(a_1)) \)

  03. If \( a=\varphi_{a_0}(a_1)\land a_0,a_1\in T_m \), return \( Max(\Omega t(a_0),\Omega t(a_1)) \)

  04. If \( a=\Omega_{a_0}\land a_0\in T_m \), return \( \Omega t(a_0) \)

  05. Else, return \( a \)


  We define the root function \( Root \) as follows:

  \( (a\in T,b\in T)\mapsto Root_b(a)\in T \)

  01. If \( a=\overline{0}\vee a=K \), return \( \overline{0} \)

  02. If \( a=a_0+a_1\land a_0\in T_s\land a_1\in T_m\setminus\{\overline{0}\} \), return \( \overline{0} \)

  03. If \( (a=\varphi_{a_0}(a_1)\vee a=\Omega_{a_0})\land a_0,a_1\in T_m \), return \( \overline{0} \)

  04. If \( b=\overline{0} \), then:

  04.01. If \( a=\Xi(a_0)\land a_0\in T_m \), return \( a \)

  04.02. If \( a=\Psi_{a_0}^{a_1}(a_2)\land a_0,a_1,a_2\in T_m \), return \( Root_b(a_0) \)

  05. If \( b\neq\overline{0} \), then:

  05.01. If \( a=\Xi(a_0)\land a_0\in T_m \), return \( \overline{0} \)

  05.02. If \( a=\Psi_{a_0}^{a_1}(a_2)\land a_0,a_1,a_2\in T_m \), then:

  05.02.01. If \( a_0=b \), return \( a \)

  05.02.02. Else if \( a_0=\Xi(a_3)\land a_3\in T_m \), return \( \overline{0} \)

  05.02.03. Else, return \( Root_b(a_0) \)

  We write \( Root(a) \) for \( Root_\overline{0}(a) \).


  We define the fixed point function \( FP \) to determine whether a term is a fixed point. Although there are no fixed points of the \( \Xi \) and \( \Psi \) functions, the idea is to determine whether for all \( a<b,f(a)<b \). Where \( a,b \) are arbitrary terms and \( f \) is an arbitrary function. For example, \( \Psi_\Omega^0(\Omega) \) is not a fixed point of the function \( \alpha\mapsto\Psi_\Omega^0(\alpha) \), but it's true that for all \( \alpha<\Psi_\Omega^0(\Omega),\Psi_\Omega^0(\alpha)<\Psi_\Omega^0(\Omega) \).

  \( (a\in T,b\in T,c\in T\cup\{0\})\mapsto FP_b^c(a)\in\{\mathbb{T},\mathbb{F}\} \) (Again, note that \( 0 \) is a numerical value that is DIFFERENT from \( \overline{0} \))

  01. If \( c=0 \), then:

  01.01. If \( t_b^0(a)=a_0+a_1\land a_0\in T_s\land a_1\in T_m\setminus\{\overline{0}\} \), return \( \mathbb{F} \)

  01.02. Else if \( \neg t_b^2(a)\in T \), return \( \mathbb{F} \)

  01.03. Else if \( b\neq K \), then:

  01.03.01. If \( t_b^0(a)\neq\Psi_{t_0}^{t_1}(t_2)\land t_0,t_1,t_2\in T_m \), return \( \mathbb{F} \)

  01.03.02. Else if \( t_b^0(a)=\Psi_{t_0}^{t_1}(t_2)\land t_0,t_1,t_2\in T_m\land t_2<t_b^2(a) \), return \( \mathbb{F} \)

  01.03.03. Else if \( t_b^0(a)<\Psi_b^\overline{0}(t_b^2(a)) \), return \( \mathbb{F} \)

  01.03.04. Else, return \( \mathbb{T} \)

  01.04. Else if \( b=K \), then:

  01.04.01. If \( (t_b^0(a)=\varphi_{t_0}(t_1)\vee t_b^0(a)=\Omega_{t_0})\land t_0,t_1\in T_m \), return \( \mathbb{F} \)

  01.04.02. Else if \( Root_b(t_b^0(a))=\Xi(r_0)\land r_0\in T_m \), then:

  01.04.02.01. If \( r_0<\Xi(t_b^2(a)) \), return \( \mathbb{F} \)

  01.04.02.02. Else if \( \Psi_{\Xi(r_0)}^\overline{0}(Suc(r_0))<\Psi_{\Xi(t_b^2(a))}^\overline{0}(Suc(t_b^2(a))) \), return \( \mathbb{F} \)

  01.04.02.03. Else, return \( \mathbb{T} \)

  02. If \( c\in T \), then:

  02.01. If \( t_K^0(c)=a_0+a_1\land a_0\in T_s\land a_1\in T_m\setminus\{\overline{0}\} \), return \( \mathbb{F} \)

  02.02. Else if \( \neg t_K^2(c)\in T \), return \( \mathbb{F} \)

  02.03. Else if \( t_K^0(c)\neq\Psi_{t_0}^{t_1}(t_2)\land t_0,t_1,t_2\in T_m \), return \( \mathbb{F} \)

  02.04. Else if \( Root_b(t_K^0(c))=\overline{0} \), return \( \mathbb{F} \)

  02.05. Else if \( Root_b(t_K^0(c))=\Psi_{r_0}^{r_1}(r_2)\land r_0,r_1,r_2\in T_m \), then:

  02.05.01. If \( r_2<a \), return \( \mathbb{F} \)

  02.05.02. Else if \( \Psi_{r_0}^\overline{0}(r_2)<\Psi_{b}^\overline{0}(a) \), return \( \mathbb{F} \)

  02.05.03. Else if \( r_2=a \), then:

  02.05.03.01. If \( r_1<t_K^2(c) \), return \( \mathbb{F} \)

  02.05.03.02. Else if \( \Psi_{r_0}^{r_1}(r_2)<\Psi_{b}^{t_K^2(c)}(a) \), return \( \mathbb{F} \)

  02.05.03.03. Else, return \( \mathbb{T} \)

  02.05.04. Else, return \( \mathbb{T} \)

  We write \( FP_b(a) \) for \( FP_b^0(a) \).


  We define the normal limit function \( NL \) for future abbreviation:

  \( (a\in T)\mapsto NL(a)\in\{\mathbb{T},\mathbb{F}\} \)

  01. If \( a=\Xi(a_0)\land a_0\in T_m \), then:

  01.01. If \( m(a)=t_K^1(m(a)) \), return \( \mathbb{F} \)

  01.02. Else if \( Cof(m(a))<\overline{\omega} \), return \( \mathbb{F} \)

  01.03. Else if \( FP_K(m(a)) \), return \( \mathbb{F} \)

  01.04. Else, return \( \mathbb{T} \)

  02. If \( a=\Psi_{a_0}^{a_1}(a_2)\land a_0,a_1,a_2\in T_m \), then:

  02.01. If \( m(a)=t_K^1(m(a)) \), return \( \mathbb{F} \)

  02.02. Else if \( Cof(m(a))<\overline{\omega} \), return \( \mathbb{F} \)

  02.03. Else if \( FP_{a_0}^{a_1}(a_2) \), return \( \mathbb{F} \)

  02.04. Else, return \( \mathbb{T} \)

  03. Else, return \( \mathbb{T} \)


  We define the normalization function \( Norm \) to normalize an ordinal to its normal form:

  \( (a\in T_m)\mapsto Norm(a)\in T \)

  01. If \( a\in T \), return \( a \)

  02. If \( \neg a\in T \), then:

  02.01. If \( a=a_0+a_1\land a_0\in T_s\land a_1\in T_m\setminus\{\overline{0}\} \), return \( Norm(a_0)+Norm(a_1) \)

  02.02. If \( a=\varphi_{a_0}(a_1)\land a_0,a_1\in T_m \), then:

  02.02.01. If \( a=a_1 \), return \( a_1 \)

  02.02.02. If \( a=a_0 \), return \( a_0 \)

  02.02.03. If \( a\neq a_0\land a\neq a_1 \), return \( a \)

  02.03. If \( a=\Omega_{a_0}\land a_0\in T_m \), return \( a_0 \)

  02.04. Else, return \( a \)

Cofinality

  We define the cofinality function \( Cof \) as follows:

  \( (a\in T)\mapsto Cof(a)\in T \)

  01. If \( a=\overline{0}\vee a=K \), return \( a \)

  02. If \( a=a_0+a_1\land a_0\in T_s\land a_1\in T_m\setminus\{\overline{0}\} \), return \( Cof(a_1) \)

  03. If \( a=\varphi_{a_0}(a_1)\land a_0,a_1\in T_m \), then:

  03.01. If \( \neg Cof(a_1)<\overline{\omega} \), return \( Cof(a_1) \)

  03.02. If \( \neg Cof(a_0)<\overline{\omega}\land Cof(a_1)<\overline{\omega} \), return \( Cof(a_0) \)

  03.03. If \( Cof(a_0)<\overline{\omega}\land Cof(a_1)<\overline{\omega}\land(a_0\neq\overline{0}\vee a_1\neq\overline{0}) \), return \( \overline{\omega} \)

  03.04. If \( a_0=\overline{0}\land a_1=\overline{0} \), return \( \overline{1} \)

  04. If \( a=\Omega_{a_0}\land a_0\in T_m \), return \( Cof(a_0) \)

  05. If \( a=\Xi(a_0)\land a_0\in T_m \), return \( a \)

  06. If \( a=\Psi_{a_0}^{a_1}(a_2)\land a_0,a_1,a_2\in T_m \), then:

  06.01. If \( a_1=\overline{0} \), return \( CP_{a_0}(a_2,\overline{0}) \)

  06.02. If \( a_1\neq\overline{0} \), return \( a \)


  We define the collapsing cofinality function \( CP \) as follows:

  \( (a\in T,b\in T,c\in T)\mapsto CP_b(a,c)\in T \)

  01. If \( a\neq t_b^1(a)\land\neg Cof(a)<\overline{\omega}\land\neg FP_b(a) \), return \( Cof(a) \)

  02. Else if \( (b=\Xi(b_0)\vee b=\Psi_{b_0}^{b_1}(b_2))\land b_0,b_1,b_2\in T_m\land NL(b)\land Cof(a)=\overline{1}\land c=\overline{0} \), return \( Cof(m(b)) \)

  03. Else if \( a=t_b^1(a)\vee \lambda(b)=a \), then:

  03.01. If \( t_b^1(a)\neq\overline{0}\land\lambda(b)<t_b^1(a)\land Cof(t_b^1(a))<b \), return \( Cof(a) \)

  03.02. Else if \( \neg(t_b^1(a)\neq\overline{0}\land\lambda(b)<t_b^1(a)) \), then:

  03.02.01. If \( b=\Xi(b_0)\land b_0\in T_m\land NL(b) \), return \( CP_d(\lambda(d),c) \), where \( d=\Xi(Add(\overline{1},t_K^1(m(b)))) \)

  03.02.02. Else if \( b=\Psi_{b_0}^{b_1}(b_2)\land b_0,b_1,b_2\in T_m\land(NL(b)\vee t_K^1(m(b))=\overline{0}) \), then:

  03.02.02.01. If \( c\neq\overline{0} \), return \( CP_{b_0}(b_2,c) \)

  03.02.02.02. If \( c=\overline{0} \), then:

  03.02.02.02.01. If \( NL(b)\vee Cof(m(b))=\overline{1} \), return \( CP_{b_0}(b_2,\Psi_{b_0}^{b_1[\overline{0}]}(b_2)) \)

  03.02.02.02.02. If \( \neg NL(b)\land Cof(m(b))\neq\overline{1} \), return \( CP_{b_0}(b_2,t_K^0(m(b))) \)

  03.02.03. Else if \( (b=\Xi(b_0)\vee b=\Psi_{b_0}^{b_1}(b_2))\land b_0,b_1,b_2\in T_m\land t_K^0(m(b))\neq\overline{0}\land Cof(t_K^1(m(b)))<K \), then:

  03.02.03.01. Return \( Cof(t_K^1(m(b))) \)

  03.02.04. Else, return \( \overline{\omega} \)

  03.03. Else, return \( \overline{\omega} \)

  04. Else if \( (b=\Omega_{b_0}\vee(t_K^1(m(b))=\overline{0}\land Cof(m(b))=\overline{1}))\land b_0\in T_m \), then:

  04.01. If \( Cof(a)=\overline{1} \), return \( CP_b(Add(\lambda(b),t_b^1(a),t_b^1(a)),\Psi_b^{m(b)[\overline{0}]}(a)) \)

  04.02. If \( Cof(a)\neq\overline{1} \), return \( CP_b(Add(\lambda(b),t_b^1(a),t_b^1(a)),t_b^0(a)) \)

  05. Else if \( (b=\Xi(b_0)\vee b=\Psi_{b_0}^{b_1}(b_2))\land NL(b)\land b_0,b_1,b_2\in T_m \), then:

  05.01. If \( Cof(a)=\overline{1} \), return \( CP_b(Add(\lambda(b),t_b^1(a),t_b^1(a)),\Psi_b^{m(b)[\overline{0}]}(a)) \)

  05.02. If \( Cof(a)\neq\overline{1} \), return \( CP_b(Add(\lambda(b),t_b^1(a),t_b^1(a)),t_b^0(a)) \)

  06. Else if \( t_K^1(m(b))=\overline{0} \), then:

  06.01. If \( Cof(a)=\overline{1} \), return \( Cof(m(b)) \)

  06.02. If \( Cof(a)\neq\overline{1} \), return \( CP_b(Add(\lambda(b),t_b^1(a),t_b^1(a)),t_b^0(a)) \)

  07. Else if \( m(b)=t_K^1(m(b))\vee Cof(m(b))=\overline{1} \), then:

  07.01. If \( Cof(t_K^1(m(b)))<K \), return \( Cof(t_K^1(m(b))) \)

  07.02. Else if \( Cof(a)=\overline{1} \), return \( \overline{\omega} \)

  07.03. Else, return \( Cof(a) \)

  08. Else if \( Cof(a)\neq\overline{1} \), return \( Cof(a) \)

  09. Else if \( c\neq\overline{0} \), return \( Cof(m(b)) \)

  10. Else if \( Cof(t_K^1(m(b)))<K \), return \( Cof(t_K^1(m(b))) \)

  11. Else, return \( \overline{\omega} \)


  We also define that \( Cof(\Gamma_{K+1})=\overline{\omega} \).

Fundamental Sequence

  We define the fundamental sequence \( [] \) as follows:

  \( (a\in T,n\in T)\mapsto a[n]\in T \)

  01. If \( a=\overline{0} \), return \( \overline{0} \)

  02. If \( a=K \), return \( n \)

  03. If \( a=a_0+a_1\land a_0\in T_s\land a_1\in T_m\setminus\{\overline{0}\} \), return \( Add(a_0,a_1[n]) \)

  04. If \( a=\varphi_{a_0}(a_1)\land a_0,a_1\in T_m \), then:

  04.01. If \( a_1=\overline{0} \), then:

  04.01.01. If \( a_0=\overline{0} \), return \( \overline{0} \)

  04.01.02. If \( Cof(a_0)=\overline{1} \), then:

  04.01.02.01. If \( n=\overline{0} \), return \( \overline{0} \)

  04.01.02.02. If \( n\neq\overline{0} \), return \( \varphi_{a_0[\overline{0}]}(a[n[\overline{0}]]) \)

  04.01.03. If \( Cof(a_0)\ge\overline{\omega} \), return \( Norm(\varphi_{a_0[n]}(\overline{0})) \)

  04.02. If \( Cof(a_1)=\overline{1} \), then:

  04.02.01. If \( a_0=\overline{0} \), then:

  04.02.01.01. If \( n=\overline{0} \), return \( \overline{0} \)

  04.02.01.02. If \( n\neq\overline{0} \), return \( Add(a[n[\overline{0}]], \varphi_\overline{0}(a_1[\overline{0}])) \)

  04.02.02. If \( Cof(a_0)=\overline{1} \), then:

  04.02.02.01. If \( n=\overline{0} \), return \( \overline{0} \)

  04.02.02.02. If \( n\neq\overline{0}\land n[\overline{0}]=\overline{0} \), return \( Norm(\varphi_{a_0}(a_1[\overline{0}])) \)

  04.02.02.03. If \( n[\overline{0}]\neq\overline{0}\land n[\overline{0}][\overline{0}]=\overline{0} \), then:

  04.02.02.03.01. If \( a_0=\overline{1} \), return \( \varphi_\overline{0}(a[n[\overline{0}]]+a[n[\overline{0}]]) \)

  04.02.02.03.02. If \( a_0\neq\overline{1} \), return \( \varphi_{a_0[\overline{0}]}(Suc(a[n[\overline{0}]])) \)

  04.02.02.04. If \( n[\overline{0}][\overline{0}]\neq\overline{0} \), return \( \varphi_{a_0[\overline{0}]}(a[n[\overline{0}]]) \)

  04.02.03. If \( Cof(a_0)\ge\overline{\omega} \), return \( \varphi_{a_0[n]}(Suc(Norm(\varphi_{a_0}(a_1[\overline{0}])))) \)

  04.03. If \( Cof(a_1)\ge\overline{\omega} \), return \( Norm(\varphi_{a_0}(a_1[n])) \)

  05. If \( a=\Omega_{a_0}\land a_0\in T_m \), then:

  05.01. If \( Cof(a_0)<\overline{\omega} \), return \( n \)

  05.02. If \( \neg Cof(a_0)<\overline{\omega} \), return \( \Omega_{a_0[n]} \)

  06. If \( a=\Xi(a_0)\land a_0\in T_m \), return \( n \)

  07. If \( a=\Psi_{a_0}^{a_1}(a_2)\land a_0,a_1,a_2\in T_m \), then:

  07.01. If \( a_1=\overline{0} \), return \( P_{a_0}(a_2,\overline{0})[n] \)

  07.02. If \( a_1\neq\overline{0} \), return \( n \)


  We define the collapsing fundamental sequence \( P[] \) as follows:

  \( (a\in T,b\in T,c\in T,n\in T)\mapsto P_b(a,c)[n]\in T \)

  01. If \( a\neq t_b^1(a)\land\neg Cof(a)<\overline{\omega}\land\neg FP_b(a) \), return \( \Psi_b^c(Add(\lambda(b),a[n],a)) \)

  02. Else if \( (b=\Xi(b_0)\vee b=\Psi_{b_0}^{b_1}(b_2))\land b_0,b_1,b_2\in T_m\land NL(b)\land Cof(a)=\overline{1}\land c=\overline{0} \), then:

  02.01. If \( \lambda(b)=a \), then:

  02.01.01. If \( b=\Xi(b_0)\land b_0\in T_m \), return \( \Xi(Add(\overline{1},m(b)[n],m(b))) \)

  02.01.02. If \( b=\Psi_{b_0}^{b_1}(b_2)\land b_0,b_1,b_2\in T_m \), return \( \Psi_{b_0}^{m(b)[n]}(b_2) \)

  02.01.03. Else, return \( \Psi_b^{m(b)[n]}(a[\overline{0}]) \)

  02.02. If \( \lambda(b)<a \), return \( \Psi_b^{m(b)[n]}(a[\overline{0}]) \)

  03. Else if \( (a=t_b^1(a)\vee\lambda(b)=a)\land t_b^1(a)\neq\overline{0}\land\lambda(b)<t_b^1(a)\land Cof(t_b^1(a))<b \), then:

  03.01. If \( t_b^0(a)=\Psi_{b_0}^{b_1}(b_2)\land b_0,b_1,b_2\in T_m\land c\neq\overline{0}\land\neg(\lambda(b_0)[\overline{0}]=t_{b_0}^0(\lambda(b_0)[\overline{0}])\land\lambda(b_0)[\overline{0}]=t_{b_0}^1(\lambda(b_0)[\overline{0}])) \), then:

  03.01.01. Let \( A=Add(\lambda(b),Add(a[\overline{0}],t_{b_0}^0(\lambda(b_0)[\overline{0}])),a),B=Add(\lambda(b),a[\overline{1}],a) \)

  03.01.02. If \( \Psi_{b}^\overline{0}(A)<\Psi_{b}^\overline{0}(B)\land A<a \), return \( \Psi_{b}^\overline{0}(Add(\lambda(b),Add(a[n],t_{b_0}^0(\lambda(b_0)[\overline{0}])),a)) \)

  03.01.03. Else if \( FP_b(a)\land c=\overline{0} \), return \( \Psi_{b}^\overline{0}(Add(\lambda(b),Add(a[n],t_b^0(a)),a)) \)

  03.01.04. Else, return \( \Psi_{b}^\overline{0}(Add(\lambda(b),Add(a[n],c),a)) \)

  03.02. Else if \( FP_b(a)\land c=\overline{0} \), return \( \Psi_{b}^\overline{0}(Add(\lambda(b),Add(a[n],t_b^0(a)),a)) \)

  03.03. Else, return \( \Psi_{b}^\overline{0}(Add(\lambda(b),Add(a[n],c),a)) \)

  04. Else if \( a=t_b^1(a)\vee\lambda(b)=a \), then:

  04.01. If \( t_b^1(a)=\overline{0}\vee\lambda(b)=t_b^1(a) \), then:

  04.01.01. If \( b=\Omega_{b_0}\land b_0\in T_m \), then:

  04.01.01.01. If \( c=\overline{0}\land b_0\neq\overline{1} \), return \( P_{\Omega_\overline{1}}(\lambda(b),Norm(\Omega_{b_0[\overline{0}]}))[n] \)

  04.01.01.02. Else if \( n=\overline{0} \), return \( c \)

  04.01.01.03. Else if \( \varphi_{P_b(Add(\lambda(b),a,a),c)[n[\overline{0}]]}(\overline{0})\in T \), return \( \varphi_{P_b(Add(\lambda(b),a,a),c)[n[\overline{0}]]}(\overline{0}) \)
